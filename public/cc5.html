Internal Parts : Flywheel

Flywheel Basics

The flywheel is a mechanical device specifically designed to efficiently store rotational energy.

  • Function: The primary function of a flywheel is to maintain a consistent rotational speed of the engine by storing energy during the power stroke and releasing it during the compression stroke.
  • Components: A typical flywheel consists of a heavy circular disc attached to the crankshaft of the engine.
  • Material: Flywheels are commonly made of cast iron, steel, or aluminum alloys, chosen for their high density and durability.
