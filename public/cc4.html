Internal Parts : Camshaft

Camshaft Basics

The camshaft is a component of an engine's valve train that is responsible for actuating the intake and exhaust valves at the proper time.

  • Function: The camshaft controls the timing and duration of the opening and closing of the engine's valves, allowing air and fuel to enter and exhaust gases to exit the combustion chamber.
  • Types: Camshafts can be classified into various types such as overhead camshaft (OHC), overhead valve (OHV), and dual overhead camshaft (DOHC), depending on their placement and configuration.
  • Components: A typical camshaft consists of lobes or cams, which are eccentric projections that push against the valves to open them, and a camshaft position sensor, which monitors the position of the camshaft for engine control purposes.
